Methodology & Assumptions

What this calculates: The monetary amount at risk for a single trade, based on the distance from entry to stop-loss, lot size, and pip value. The result is also expressed as a percentage of total account balance.

Assumptions: This calculation assumes a fixed pip value per lot and does not account for slippage, spread widening, or gap risk. Actual risk in volatile market conditions may exceed the calculated value.

Common guideline: Many capital preservation approaches recommend limiting risk per trade to 1–2% of account balance. Values above 5% are typically considered aggressive and may warrant review.
